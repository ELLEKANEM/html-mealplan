THE PLAN

GOALS

  • Increase Fiber: Fiber helps lower cholesterol levels. Incorporate whole grains, granola, vegetables, fruits, and legumes.
  • Healthy Fats: Use unsaturated fats like olive oil, avocado, and nuts. Avoid trans fats and limit saturated fats.
  • Lean Proteins: Choose lean meats, fish, and plant-based proteins like beans and lentils.
  • Limit Sodium and Sugars: Reduce salty and sugary foods to maintain overall cardiovascular health.

Daily Meal Plan

Monday

  • Breakfast: Oats topped with sliced bananas and a handful of groundnuts.
  • Lunch: Rice(preferably brown) with steamed veggies (broccoli, carrots, spinach) and grilled mackerel.
  • Snack: A small bowl of sliced fruits.
  • Dinner: Boiled yam with efo riro and fish.

Tuesday

  • Breakfast: Whole grain cereal with low-fat milk and a side of an apple or Airfried Plantain with eggs
  • Lunch: Ofada rice with fish stew (use minimal oil) and a side of steamed green veggies.
  • Snack: A handful of unsalted almonds/cashews.
  • Dinner: Moi Moi with a small portion of grilled chicken and a side salad.

Wednesday

  • Breakfast: Akamu (pap) with a small portion of moin moin.
  • Lunch: Jollof rice, served with grilled turkey and coleslaw.
  • Snack: Fresh fruit salad (watermelon, mango, pineapple, and oranges).
  • Dinner: Roasted plantain with a small portion of ewa agoyin (mashed beans) and a vegetable stir-fry.

Thursday

  • Breakfast: Scrambled eggs with saut√©ed tomatoes and onions, served with whole-grain bread.
  • Lunch: Sweet potato/plantain porridge with a small portion of grilled fish and steamed veggies.
  • Snack: A handful of groundnut or cashews.
  • Dinner: Okra soup with a small portion of wheat or oat flour and lean beef.

Friday

  • Breakfast: Greek yogurt, granola with sliced cucumbers and tomatoes on the side.
  • Lunch: Boiled wateryam with a small portion of edikangikong soup(vegetable soup) (using minimal palm oil) and fish.
  • Snack: A small bowl of sliced mangoes.
  • Dinner: couscous with vegetable stir-fry and grilled chicken.

Saturday

  • Breakfast: Lettuce bacon tomatoes and egg stuffing
  • Lunch: Amala and ewedu with small portion of gbegiri.
  • Snack: A small bowl of fruits.
  • Dinner: Grilled tilapia with a side of mixed vegetables and a small portion of boiled potatoes.

Sunday

  • Breakfast: Pancakes with sardines (or a drizzle of honey if you don't like sardines) and a side of fresh fruit.
  • Lunch: Basmati rice with stew and beef.
  • Snack: A handful of dried fruits (like dates or raisins).
  • Dinner: porridge beans and boiled plantain.
